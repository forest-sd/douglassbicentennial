About the Bicentennial

The Frederick Douglass Bicentennial celebrates the 200th anniversary of the birth of Frederick Douglass (born Frederick Augustus Washington Bailey, c. February 1818), one of the most influential Americans in history.

Who Was Frederick Douglass?

Born into slavery in Talbot County, Maryland, Douglass escaped bondage and became one of the foremost voices for abolition, women's suffrage, and human rights. His autobiographies, including "Narrative of the Life of Frederick Douglass," remain among the most powerful accounts of the American experience.

The Bicentennial Project

Our project brings together scholars, educators, community leaders, and citizens to honor Douglass's legacy through events, educational programs, publications, and community engagement. We seek to connect his historic fight for justice with contemporary struggles for equality.

Our Goals

  • Raise awareness of Douglass's life, writings, and political activism
  • Create educational resources for schools and community organizations
  • Host events that connect Douglass's legacy to current civil rights issues
  • Build a lasting community of individuals committed to justice and equality
